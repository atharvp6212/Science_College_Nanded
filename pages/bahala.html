Palash (Flame of the Forest)

Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Cassia fistula, commonly known as the Golden Shower Tree or Bahala, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent. Recognizable for its pendulous clusters of bright yellow flowers, it is widely cultivated for its ornamental beauty. The tree produces distinctive, long, cylindrical seed pods that give it the name "Golden Shower" due to their appearance. In addition to its aesthetic value, Cassia fistula has medicinal uses in traditional systems of medicine. The pulp from the seed pods is used for its laxative properties. The Golden Shower Tree is a symbol of cultural significance in various regions and is often associated with festivals and celebrations. Its hard, durable wood has applications in construction and carpentry. Cassia fistula is not only admired for its visual appeal but also holds practical and cultural importance.
