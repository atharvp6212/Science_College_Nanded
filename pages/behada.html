Baheda

Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Terminalia bellirica, commonly known as Bibhitaki or Bahera, is a large deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, particularly the Indian subcontinent. Belonging to the Combretaceae family, Bibhitaki plays a crucial role in Ayurvedic medicine as one of the three components of Triphala, renowned for its digestive benefits. The tree has distinctive exfoliating bark, and its wood is prized for its durability. The name "Bibhitaki," translating to "fearless" in Sanskrit, adds an interesting cultural dimension to its significance. Beyond its medicinal use, Bibhitaki's wood finds practical applications in construction, reflecting the tree's versatile nature in traditional and practical domains.
