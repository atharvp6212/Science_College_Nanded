Cycas (Sago Palm) - Male

Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Cycas revoluta, commonly known as the Sago Palm or cycus male plant, is a slow-growing and attractive cycad native to Japan. The Sago Palm is not a true palm but rather a cycad with a distinctive crown of feathery, fern-like leaves that arise from a central, stout trunk. It is a popular ornamental plant in gardens and indoor spaces due to its elegant appearance and tolerance to various environmental conditions. Despite its name, the Sago Palm is not a true palm and belongs to a primitive group of plants known as cycads. It is appreciated for its low maintenance and resilience, making it a favored choice for landscape design and as a potted plant. However, it's important to note that all parts of the Sago Palm are toxic if ingested.
