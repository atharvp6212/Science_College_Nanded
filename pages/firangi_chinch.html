Pithecellobium dulce

Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Pithecellobium dulce, commonly known as Manila Tamarind or Firangi Chinch, is a small to medium-sized evergreen tree native to Central and South America. It is recognized for its dense, umbrella-shaped canopy and compound leaves. The tree produces flat, twisted pods containing sweet and sour pulp, which is edible. The seeds are used for various traditional purposes. Manila Tamarind is often cultivated as an ornamental tree for its attractive foliage and fragrant flowers. It's valued in agroforestry for its nitrogen-fixing properties, contributing to soil fertility. In traditional medicine, different parts of the tree are used for various remedies.
