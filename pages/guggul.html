गुग्गुळ

Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Commiphora caudata, commonly known as Indian Bdellium or Guggul, is a small deciduous tree or shrub native to India and other parts of South Asia. It belongs to the Burseraceae family and is valued for its resin, which has been used in traditional Ayurvedic medicine for centuries. The resin, known as Guggul or Guggulu, is obtained from the bark and is believed to have various health benefits, particularly in promoting joint and cardiovascular health. Guggul has been traditionally used for its anti-inflammatory and lipid-lowering properties. The resin is also employed as incense in some cultural and religious practices. Commiphora caudata represents a significant botanical resource in traditional medicine and continues to be studied for its potential pharmaceutical applications.
