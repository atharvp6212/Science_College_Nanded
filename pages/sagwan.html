I am Tectona grandis, commonly known as Teak, is a large deciduous tree native to South and Southeast Asia, but it has been widely cultivated in various tropical regions. Renowned for its high-quality timber, teak is one of the most valuable and sought-after hardwoods globally. The wood is durable, resistant to decay, and possesses natural oils that make it suitable for a range of applications, including shipbuilding, outdoor furniture, and flooring. Teak is also appreciated for its attractive, straight-grained appearance and natural resistance to pests. The tree has been extensively planted in teak plantations for sustainable timber production. Additionally, teak has cultural significance and is sometimes used in traditional medicine. Its economic importance, coupled with its adaptability and valuable timber, makes Tectona grandis a notable species in forestry and woodworking industries.
