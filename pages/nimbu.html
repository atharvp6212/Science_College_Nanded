Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Citrus aurantiifolia, commonly known as Key lime or Mexican lime, is a small, evergreen tree belonging to the Rutaceae family. Native to Southeast Asia, it is now cultivated in various tropical and subtropical regions worldwide. The tree produces small, round, greenish-yellow fruits known as Key limes, which are valued for their distinctive flavor. Key limes are a rich source of vitamin C and are commonly used in culinary applications, including beverages, desserts, and as a flavoring agent in a variety of dishes. The tree itself has glossy, dark green leaves, and fragrant white flowers. Key limes are known for their refreshing taste and are integral to the production of Key lime pie, a popular dessert. Additionally, the essential oil extracted from the fruit and leaves is used in perfumery and aromatherapy. The adaptability, culinary versatility, and aromatic qualities of Citrus aurantiifolia make it a valuable and cherished plant in various cultures.
