Fig

Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Ficus racemosa, commonly known as the Cluster Fig or Umbar, is a deciduous tree native to the Indian subcontinent and Southeast Asia. Recognizable by its distinctive clustered fig fruits, it grows in a variety of habitats, including forests and riverbanks. The leaves are ovate, and the fruits are small, green figs that grow in dense clusters along the branches. In Hindu mythology, the Cluster Fig is associated with the Nyagrodha tree, believed to host benevolent spirits. The tree has cultural significance and is often planted near temples. Additionally, various parts of the tree have applications in traditional medicine for treating conditions like diabetes and skin disorders. The Cluster Fig serves as a valuable resource for wildlife, providing food for birds and animals. It is a versatile and culturally significant species with ecological importance.
