Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Terminalia chebula, commonly known as Haritaki or Hirada, is a large deciduous tree native to Southeast Asia, including the Indian subcontinent. As a key component of the Ayurvedic formulation Triphala, Haritaki holds a significant place in traditional medicine for its digestive and detoxifying properties. The tree features elliptic, sub-opposite leaves and greenish-yellow flowers. Haritaki is recognized for its adaptability, thriving in diverse environments. Beyond its medicinal uses, the fruit of Terminalia chebula has been traditionally employed in dyeing and tanning processes. With a rich history in Ayurveda, Haritaki continues to be valued for its potential health benefits and versatile applications in various cultural practices.
