Custard Apple

Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Annona squmosa, commonly known as Sugar Apple or Custard Apple, is a small deciduous tree native to tropical Americas. Its distinctive round fruits, characterized by a scaly exterior and sweet, white pulp punctuated with dark seeds, are popular for their delightful taste. Widely used in fresh consumption, the fruit also features in beverages, ice creams, and desserts across tropical cuisines. Beyond its culinary appeal, various parts of the plant hold significance in traditional medicine, believed to possess therapeutic properties. The Sugar Apple stands out not only for its flavorful contribution to gastronomy but also for its potential in traditional healing practices.
