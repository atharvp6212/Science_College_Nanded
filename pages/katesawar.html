Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Ceiba pentandra, commonly known as the Kapok tree or Silk-cotton tree, is a large deciduous tree native to the tropical rainforests of the Americas. Recognizable for its massive size and distinctive trunk with buttress roots, the Kapok tree can reach heights of over 200 feet. The tree produces large, woody seed pods filled with silky fibers that surround the seeds. These fibers, known as kapok, have traditionally been used as a natural filling for pillows, mattresses, and life jackets due to their buoyancy and water-resistant properties. Kapok trees are ecologically important, providing habitat for various species, and they play a role in traditional medicine in some cultures. Additionally, the Kapok tree is often revered in local folklore and may have cultural and spiritual significance in certain regions.
