Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Nyctanthes arbor-tristis, commonly known as the Night-flowering Jasmine or Parijat, is a small, deciduous tree or shrub native to Southeast Asia. Recognized for its fragrant and delicate flowers, Parijat blooms at night and sheds its flowers during the early morning. The flowers are small, with white petals and orange tubes, creating a visually appealing contrast. In Hindu mythology, Parijat is associated with various legends, and the flowers are considered sacred, often used in religious ceremonies. The tree has traditional uses in Ayurvedic medicine, where different parts are believed to have medicinal properties. The distinctive and fragrant flowers, coupled with cultural and mythological significance, make Nyctanthes arbor-tristis a valued ornamental and spiritual plant in many tropical regions.
