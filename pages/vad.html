Banyan

Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Ficus benghalensis, the Banyan tree, is a large fig tree native to the Indian subcontinent known for its expansive canopy and aerial prop roots. Sacred in Hindu mythology and associated with Vishnu, it starts as an epiphyte, growing on structures, developing interconnected trunks. The Great Banyan in Kolkata is famed as the world's widest tree. With historical significance and ecological roles, including providing habitat and preventing soil erosion, Banyan trees are also planted for ornamental value, symbolizing strength, longevity, and interconnectedness in diverse cultures.
