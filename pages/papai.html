Hi! Welcome to Science College, Nanded. I am Carica papaya, commonly known as Papaya, is a tropical fruit tree native to Central America and Mexico. The tree produces large, elongated fruits with a yellow to orange hue when ripe. The juicy, orange flesh has a sweet taste, and while the central cavity contains edible black seeds, they are typically discarded. Papaya is widely enjoyed fresh, either on its own, in fruit salads, or used in various culinary creations such as smoothies, juices, and desserts. The presence of the enzyme papain in the fruit adds to its nutritional value and makes it a versatile and popular choice in tropical and subtropical regions.
